## Background

AI Career Radar produces gap reports that quote real JDs from
`src/data/web_bundle.json` (443 records) — each record has fields
`id · company · title · location · source_url · seniority · archetype ·
years_min · canonical_skills · raw_skills · body`. The `body` field
holds the source JD text that any quoted span in the report must
match. The prompt (`src/lib/prompts.ts`) instructs the model to:
- quote language from specific JDs with `jd_id / company` tag
- end the report with an "evidence appendix" listing `jd_id + company
  + title only`.

Current baselines validate structure + capture + Evidence Appendix
**presence** + fixture-specific keyword alignment + must-not-happen
absence + operational limits. They do **not** validate:
- whether quoted text was actually copied from the cited JD
- whether the cited jd_id is real
- whether the company/title metadata match the record
- whether quotes are attributed to the right JD
- whether Evidence Appendix items are actually referenced by claims

Since the product's trust story depends on "we cite real JDs, we
don't make things up," quote integrity is the highest-leverage next
quality gate before expanding to Fixtures C/D/E.
